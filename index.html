    Platform building blocks

    Phone runs the logic. TV renders the output.

    ullr splits the experience across two screens. Your app logic, user input, and session state live entirely on the phone. The TV is a shared display — it renders what the phone sends, in real time. This means no app on the TV, no special hardware, and no SDK required on the big screen.

    Explore architecture
    • Phone — runs game loop, holds session state, handles input
    • TV — displays a rendered stream, no logic required
    • Server — coordinates pairing and relays events
    • No TV SDK — any HDMI-connected screen works

    From QR scan to live session in seconds.

    The connection flow is the backbone of every ullr experience. A user scans a QR code on the TV, their phone authenticates, and a secure real-time channel opens. From that point your app sends events and the TV updates instantly — with no manual configuration on either side.

    See the full flow
    • Step 1 — TV displays session QR code
    • Step 2 — Phone scans and authenticates
    • Step 3 — Real-time channel opens
    • Step 4 — App starts sending events

    Design for the couch, not the desk.

    TV screens are viewed from 2–4 metres. Type must be larger, contrast must be higher, and interactions must be forgiving. ullr experiences live in living rooms, so every design decision should account for distance, shared visibility, and the phone as the primary input surface.

    Read design guide
    • Min 32px — body text size on TV
    • 4.5:1 contrast — minimum for readability at distance
    • Phone = input — TV is always display-only
    • Big tap targets — minimum 48×48px on phone
